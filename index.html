Lirienna - Branding Project

Ã—

Brand concept This project was developed as part of a class assignment focused on creating a product that was national, realistic, ecological, and environmentally friendly. For this purpose, I designed a series of aroma diffusers with floral essences imported from Italy to Spain.
Each diffuser serves as a tribute to iconic women from the world of art, including music, cinema, and painting. The chosen figures were Sharon Tate, Marilyn Monroe, and Amy Winehouse, each represented through a specific color that reflects their essence: pink for Marilyn Monroe, turquoise for Sharon Tate and olive for Amy Winehouse.
All products are made with recycled materials and are biodegradable. When special purchases are made for celebrations such as birthdays or Christmas, we use special packaging that includes a handmade bow, small flowers that are related to the scent of the purchased fragrance, and a personalized card on which the customer can write whatever they want.
The values of the brand are handicraft, passion, sensuality and avant-garde. The typographies used in this project are Gowun Batang Bold, Exmouth and Inter Regular.
Naming LIRIENNA is a name that evokes delicacy, elegance, and poetry. Its soft, melodic sound suggests something ethereal and feminine, like a scented whisper. Derived from the lily flowerâ€”a universal symbol of purity, beauty, and spiritualityâ€”the name is closely linked to the floral and sensorial universe that defines the brand. Furthermore, the ending "-enna" adds a lyrical, Italian-inspired touch, reinforcing the sophisticated and timeless dimension of home fragrance.
Logo The brand logo is pressure printed on the labels and banners, in such a way that each one of them plays with depth in an elegant way.

Silbicortipartico - Font creating

Ã—

Project description Silbicortipartico is a typography developed as an academic project, created from scratch with the aim of reinterpreting the Art Deco aesthetics in a contemporary proposal. Its design is characterized by the elegance of elongated shapes, the use of right angles combined with delicate curves and a clear intention to maintain a refined, simple and sophisticated style.
Thanks to its versatility, it is suitable for branding and stationery applications, establishing itself as an updated and stylized version of classic Art Deco. This project allowed me to develop deeper into type construction, improve my understanding of letter anatomy, and explore how typography alone can communicate personality, historical context, and aesthetic sensibility.
Why Silbicortipartico? The typographyÂ´s name is meant to evoke a sense of timeless elegance, just like its design. The name is inspired by a passage from the book Alice Through the Looking Glass and What She Found There by Lewis Carroll, more specifically from a poem that Alice finds near the beginning of the story, in which, the vorpal sword that Alice would later use in the plot, is mentioned.
In popular culture, it often refers to an unusually curved sword, which I think accurately reflects what my typography is visually like.
Creativity process To create this typography, I played with two fundamental elements: a curved line and a straight line. By exploring the relationship between these two simple forms, I was able to establish a consistent visual language that guided the entire design process. With just these elements, I constructed a wide variety of characters, ranging from uppercase and lowercase letters to punctuation marks and numerals.
This limitation not only challenged me to think creatively about proportion, rhythm, and balance, but also gave the typography distinctive personality rooted in geometric simplicity and structural coherence.

Sofia Coppola Fanzine - Editorial design

Ã—

Project description This is a personal project in which I developed my own interpretation of the book Sofia Coppola Archives. Sofia Coppola is my favorite film director, and I wanted to pay tribute to her through this work.
The zine brings together all of Coppola's films, presenting a brief synopsis of each and describing relevant aspects of the filming process. In addition, each film features a collage of behind-the-scenes photographs, which adds a visual dimension to the content.
My goal was to reflect the characteristic femininity that the director imprints on her works, whether in teenage characters or adult women. For this reason, I opted for a pink cover that adds a touch of innocence, a recurring color in the protagonists of her stories.
The fonts used are Anastasia Script for the titles and Garamond for the body.
